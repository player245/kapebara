About Capybaras

Capybaras

Capybaras are the largest rodents in the world. They have a stout, barrel-shaped body with a head that resembles that of a guinea pig. They typically measure around 1.2 meters (4 feet) in length and stand about 2 feet all at the shoulder. Adult capybaras can weigh between 35 to 65 kilograms. They have short, coarse fur that can vary in color from reddish-brown to grayish-brown. Capybaras are herbivores with a primarily vegetarian diet. They feed on a variety of plant materials, including grasses, aquatic plants, fruits, bark, and tender shoots. They have specialized incisors and cheek teeth designed for grazing and chewing plant matter. Capybaras are also known to engage in a behavior called coprophagy, where they consume their own feces as a way to extract additional nutrients from their food.
